More about waterfall

A waterfall is a natural flowing body of water that drops vertically or nearly vertically from a higher elevation to a lower elevation,
often forming a plunge pool at the base.
Waterfalls are a fascinating geographical feature that can be found in various parts of the world,
shaping the surrounding landscape and creating unique ecosystems.

Waterfalls are formed when a stream or river flows over a steep drop in the landscape, often created by tectonic activity, erosion, or volcanic activity. The water flows over the edge of the drop,
creating a vertical or near-vertical fall. The height of the waterfall can vary greatly,
ranging from a few meters to hundreds of meters.
